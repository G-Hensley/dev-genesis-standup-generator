============================================================================
ADR (Architecture Decision Record) USAGE GUIDE
============================================================================

WHAT IS AN ADR?
An Architecture Decision Record captures an important architectural decision
along with its context and consequences.

WHEN TO CREATE AN ADR?
- Choosing between technologies/frameworks
- Defining system architecture patterns
- Making significant structural changes
- Decisions that are hard to reverse
- Decisions that affect multiple team members

NAMING CONVENTION:
ADR-001-short-title.md
ADR-002-another-decision.md

LIFECYCLE:
1. Proposed - Under discussion
2. Accepted - Approved and implemented
3. Deprecated - No longer relevant
4. Superseded - Replaced by another ADR

TIPS:
- Keep ADRs concise but complete
- Focus on the "why" not just the "what"
- Document trade-offs honestly
- Link related ADRs together
- Update status when decisions change

RESOURCES:

- https://cognitect.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions
